## Step 2: AI Tool Selection Criteria

Once current state analysis is complete, select the optimal AI tool for your organization.

Many companies tend to choose "feature-rich tools," but what matters is **"whether it addresses your company's challenges."**

:::message
**3 Key Points for Tool Selection**

- **Data Connectivity**: Can it seamlessly connect to existing CRM, MA, email, and call logs?
- **Scalability and Transparency**: Are scoring rationales explainable and adjustable to business rules?
- **Implementation Speed and Operational Cost**: Are PoC to production timelines and ongoing operational costs realistic?

:::

Also include privacy and compliance, and data retention policies in evaluation criteria.

AI black boxes easily breed field distrust and cause adoption resistance. Choose tools that can explain scoring rationales.

:::alert
**Caution**: Don't evaluate tools based on feature demos alone; we recommend conducting PoC (30-90 days) with actual data.

Demo environment success doesn't necessarily reproduce in production environments.

:::

## Step 4: Optimize Priorities with AI Scoring

Once automated data collection is on track, optimize sales priorities with "AI scoring."

### What is AI Scoring?

A mechanism where AI automatically calculates close probability for each deal by combining lead attributes, behavioral history, and past order patterns.

It's the most powerful tool for concentrating limited sales resources on high-probability deals.

### Success Points

Visualize scoring rationale in a form understandable to the operational team.

When you can explain "why this deal is A-rank," sales rep behavior change occurs more easily and AI trust increases.

:::message
**Operational Tip**: Initially set conservative thresholds to avoid over-prioritization and verify accuracy.

How reliable is AI scoring accuracy?

AI scoring accuracy improves over time as the system learns from your historical sales data.

**Accuracy progression**:
- **Initial implementation (Month 1)**: 60-70% accuracy (baseline, trained on historical data)
- **After 3 months**: 75-82% accuracy (AI learns from new win/loss patterns)
- **After 6 months**: 85-90% accuracy (mature model with sufficient training data)
- **Traditional manual scoring**: 60-68% accuracy (static rules, per Forrester research)

**What makes AI scoring reliable**:
1. **Continuous learning**: Every closed deal (won or lost) trains the model to improve predictions
2. **Multi-signal analysis**: Analyzes 50-200 data points (vs. 5-10 for manual scoring)
3. **Explainable AI**: Shows *why* a lead scored high/low (e.g., "Similar companies closed in 30 days", "Decision-maker engaged", "Budget confirmed")
4. **Human-in-the-loop**: Sales reps can override AI scores and provide feedback, improving future predictions

**Best practice**: Use AI scoring as **"reference information"** combined with sales rep experience, not as absolute truth. Choose tools that visualize scoring rationale to build trust with your team.

**Optifai transparency**: Dashboard shows score breakdown (firmographic 30 points, behavioral 25 points, intent 20 points, etc.) so reps understand the "why" behind each lead priority.
